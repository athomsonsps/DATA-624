The project includes an Executive Summary that gives a short overview of the forecasting work for Lone Star Appliances and Texas HealthLink.

Exercise #1: Lone Star Appliances forecasts quarterly appliance shipments using time series models. It focuses on shipment growth, seasonality, COVID disruption, and planning needs.

Exercise #1 Technical Work includes R code, plots, STL decomposition, ETS, ARIMA, regression, seasonal naive modeling, model comparison, final forecasts, and residual checks.

Exercise #1 Recommendation gives business guidance for production, staffing, inventory, logistics, and risk planning.

Exercise #2: Texas HealthLink forecasts monthly patient visits for five clinics and the full system. It focuses on staffing, budgeting, scheduling, and clinic capacity.

Exercise #2 Technical Work includes clinic-level forecasts, systemwide forecasts, model comparison, plots, and forecast tables.

Exercise #2 Recommendation explains how HealthLink should use the clinic-level and systemwide forecasts for planning.

Bonus #2 adds interview-ready time series explanations, graph commentary, and mock Q&A in plain language.

The Final Conclusion wraps up the project and connects the forecasting results back to business decisions.

The References section includes course/project sources and forecasting textbooks.
